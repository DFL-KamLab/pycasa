Dear Alex,

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to express my sincere
appreciation for your SORT (Simple Online and Realtime Tracking) implementation
available at https://github.com/abewley/sort. It is genuinely one of the most
elegant and accessible multi-object tracking solutions out there -- clean,
well-documented, and remarkably effective for its simplicity. It has been an
invaluable resource for our work.

We are using SORT as part of a Python library called PyCASA, which performs
cell tracking and analysis for microscopy data. While integrating SORT, we
discovered a small edge case: when a video frame contains zero detections, the
original _iou_batch function raises an error due to a NumPy broadcasting issue
on empty arrays. We patched this with a two-line early-return guard at the top
of _iou_batch that returns a zero-shaped IoU matrix when either input is empty.
It is a minimal, non-breaking fix.

We noticed that the repository was last updated approximately 6 years ago. If
SORT is still actively maintained, we would love to submit a pull request with
this fix -- it is a simple change that could help others who encounter the same
issue. If the project is no longer actively maintained, that is completely
understandable, and we just wanted to reach out and say thank you.

Either way, your work on SORT has had a real impact and continues to be widely
used and appreciated by the research community. Thank you for making it
available.
